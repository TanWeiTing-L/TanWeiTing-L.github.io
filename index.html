Here's what you need to know about the internet

The internet is a vast global network that connects billions of devices, enabling the exchange of information and communication worldwide. It operates through interconnected servers, routers, and devices using standardized protocols like TCP/IP. Understanding the internet involves recognizing its many facets: it hosts the World Wide Web, which provides access to websites and online services; it facilitates instant communication through email, messaging apps, and social media; and it serves as a hub for entertainment, education, and commerce. However, users should also be aware of potential risks, such as privacy concerns, cybersecurity threats, and misinformation, emphasizing the importance of practicing safe and responsible online behavior.

Here's what you actually need to know

  • The internet connects computers and devices all over the world.
  • It helps you communicate, learn, work, and have fun.
  • You can use it for things like watching videos, playing games, or finding information.
  • Be careful of risks like hackers, scams, and false information.
  • Stay safe by protecting your data and thinking critically about what you see.

Hold on... Hackers?!

Yup! Here's a few tips on how to maintain internet safety

  • 1. Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • 2. Think before clicking links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
  • 3. Share personal information carefully, especially on social media.
  • 4. Keep software and devices updated to protect against security threats.
